socialmedia

Definition: Social media are digital platforms that allow users to create, share, and interact with content and communities online.

Examples:Popular platforms include Facebook, Instagram, X (Twitter), TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, and Snapchat.

Communication :Enables instant global communication, connecting people across different cultures and locations

Content Creation: Users can share photos, videos, stories, live streams, and text posts easily.

Marketing Tool : Businesses use social media for branding, advertising, customer engagement, and sales.

whats'app

Definition: WhatsApp is a free instant messaging app that allows users to send text messages, voice messages, images, videos, and documents over the internet.

Ownership:Founded in 2009 by Brian Acton and Jan Koum, it was acquired by Facebook (now Meta Platforms) in 2014.

Global Popularity :Used by over 2 billion people worldwide, making it one of the most popular messaging apps.

End-to-End Encryption: All messages, calls, photos, and videos are end-to-end encrypted, ensuring only the sender and recipient can read them.

Voice & Video Calls:Supports free voice and video calls over Wi-Fi or mobile data, both one-on-one and group calls.

Groups & Communities:Users can create group chats and now Communities, allowing large-scale communication and coordination.

instagram

Definition:Instagram is a social media platform focused on photo and video sharing, allowing users to post visual content and interact through likes, comments, and messages.

Launch & Ownership: Created in 2010 by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger, and later acquired by Facebook (Meta Platforms) in 2012.

User Base:Has over 2 billion monthly active users globally, making it one of the most popular social networking apps.

Features : Includes Posts, Stories, Reels, IGTV, and Live for different types of content sharing

Stories:Short-lived posts (photos or videos) that disappear after 24 hours, similar to Snapchat's feature.

twitter

Definition:Twitter, now rebranded as X, is a social media platform where users post and interact through short messages called “tweets.”

Founding:Launched in 2006 by Jack Dorsey, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams.

Ownership :In 2022, Elon Musk acquired Twitter and began transforming it into X, aiming to make it an “everything app.”

Real-Time Updates: Known for instant news sharing, trending topics, and real-time global discussions

Verification System :The blue checkmark (Twitter Blue / X Premium) indicates verified or paying users.

facebook

Definition:Facebook is a social networking platform that allows users to connect, share, and communicate through posts, photos, videos, and messages.

Founding :Created in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg and his college roommates at Harvard University.

Ownership:Operated by Meta Platforms, Inc., which also owns Instagram, WhatsApp, and Threads.

User Base:Has over 3 billion monthly active users, making it the largest social media platform in the world.

phone pay

Definition : PhonePe is a digital payment and financial services app that allows users to send and receive money instantly using the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) system in India.

Launch : Founded in 2015 by Sameer Nigam, Rahul Chari, and Burzin Engineer.

Ownership : PhonePe is owned by Walmart, after being acquired through Flipkart, which is also Walmart-owned.

UPI Payments : Users can link their bank accounts and make instant transfers using UPI without needing bank details—just a UPI ID or mobile number.

Services : Supports mobile recharges, bill payments, insurance, gold purchase, mutual funds, and loan services.
