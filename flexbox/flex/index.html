social media

Refers to platforms and tools used to share content, ideas, and information.

Focuses on content creation and distribution (text, images, videos

The main activity is publishing or broadcasting information.

It's about media — the “what” that is shared.

Used for marketing, brand awareness, and content promotion.

Users are often followers or audiences consuming content

Emphasizes reach and visibility.

One-way communication is common (creator → audience).

Social network

Refers to the connections and relationships among people or groups on these platforms.

Focuses on building and maintaining relationships between users.

Examples: Facebook, LinkedIn, online communities, or any network of people

The main activity is interacting, networking, and engaging with others.

It's about networking — the “who” shares and connects.

Used for relationship-building, collaboration, and communication.

Users are participants or members forming part of a social circle or group.

Emphasizes interaction and community.

web relationship

A social network links individuals, groups, or organizations based on shared interests, goals, or relationships.

Relationships are two-way — people communicate, respond, and engage with one another regularly.

Networks enable members to share knowledge, opinions, and updates quickly across connections

Over time, consistent interaction fosters trust, credibility, and stronger personal or professional bonds.

Networks often form around shared passions, professions, or causes (e.g., gamers, educators, entrepreneurs

Members help each other — offering emotional support, advice, or collaboration on projects.

The web has nodes (people) and links (connections), forming patterns like clusters, hubs, and communities

Ideas, trends, and behaviors spread through these relationships — influential members can shape others' opinions

Effective networks depend on give-and-take — users contribute as much as they benefit.
