About cats

Cats are natural hunters with sharp claws, quick reflexes, and the ability to run up to 30 miles per hour.

They have a keen sense of smell and hearing, able to detect higher frequencies than humans

While they mainly meow at humans, they use body language, purring, and a variety of other sounds (over 100) to communicate with each other and with people

Cats have a flexible spine and a "righting reflex" that allows them to almost always land on their feet when they fall, a trait aided by their lack of a collarbone and flexible ears.

They spend about 70% of their day sleeping, often 14-16 hours, to conserve energy for hunting.

About dogs
